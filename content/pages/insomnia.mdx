# What is Insomnia?

Insomnia is a sleep disorder that makes it hard to fall asleep or stay asleep, even when you have the opportunity to sleep. It can cause daytime problems like fatigue, mood changes, and difficulty concentrating.

</Header>

## Common symptoms

The following are some common behaviors for people with Insomnia. They do not apply to all people, as the condition is individual and varies from person to person.

<Definition>
  <DefinitionItem summary="Difficulty falling asleep">
    Even when exhausted, people with insomnia may lie awake for hours, unable to drift off.
  </DefinitionItem>
  <DefinitionItem summary="Waking up often">
    Sleep may be interrupted by frequent awakenings during the night, making it hard to feel rested.
  </DefinitionItem>
  <DefinitionItem summary="Daytime fatigue">
    A lack of quality sleep often leads to feeling drained, sluggish, or sleepy throughout the day.
  </DefinitionItem>
  <DefinitionItem summary="Irritability or mood swings">
    Sleep deprivation can make emotions harder to regulate, leading to frustration or sadness.
  </DefinitionItem>
  <DefinitionItem summary="Trouble concentrating">
    Mental fog or difficulty focusing on tasks is common, as the brain struggles without proper rest.
  </DefinitionItem>
  <DefinitionItem summary="Waking up too early">
    Some people with insomnia wake up well before they intend to and can’t fall back asleep.
  </DefinitionItem>
  <DefinitionItem summary="Anxiety about sleep">
    Worrying about not sleeping well can create a vicious cycle, making it even harder to relax and rest.
  </DefinitionItem>
  <DefinitionItem summary="Physical tension">
    Restlessness, muscle tightness, or discomfort can keep the body from settling down for sleep.
  </DefinitionItem>
</Definition>

</section>

<section>

## What can I do?

1. **Stop joking about Insomnia** These jokes and memes are often insensitive and hurtful to people with the condition. By joking, you’re spreading misinformation and disrespecting people with Insomnia.

2. **Spread awareness.**  Next time you hear a person using the word Insomnia incorrectly or having a stereotypical view of people with Insomnia, send them this page.

3. **Support groups for Insomnia.** There are many organizations that raise money to support people with Insomnia and their families. Even if you can’t support them yourself, you can help share these groups with others, so they can support these groups.

</section>
